Start of M.T.D

The idea of our network intrusion program traces back to a pivotal moment when one of our team members, still in high school at the time, found himself immersed in the world of online gaming. While indulging in flash games on a website inundated with intrusive advertisements, an unexpected disruption occurred. His energetic dog stirred up a commotion in the dinnig room, casuing him to lose track of what his cursor was clicking. Upon returning his focus to the laptop, he discovered a multitude of new windows had materialized, and to his dismay, his mouse cursor seemed paralyzed, trapped by an unseen force. It was a jarring realization that his laptop had fallen prey to a malicious attack, rendering it virtually unusable. Despite attempts to salvage his system, the damage proved irreversible, causing him to pruchase a new laptop. This event served as a catalyst, propelling him into the realm of computer security and learn about cyber threats and how to defend against them.

Evolution of M.T.D

Initially focused on identifying and mitigating network threats, the project expanded its scope to incorporate the detection and classification of malicious software. This evolution involved augmenting the existing network intrusion detection capabilities with additional features and algorithms specifically tailored to identify malware within network traffic and files. The expansion into malware classification and detection required a multifaceted approach, including deepening the understanding of malware behaviors, collecting datasets containing samples of both benign and malicious software, and implementing sophisticated machine learning models. By integrating these new components into the existing system, the project gained the ability to not only detect and respond to network intrusions but also to identify and categorize various types of malware, thereby enhancing its overall threat detection and mitigation capabilities in the ever-evolving landscape of cybersecurity.
